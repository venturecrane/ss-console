# Outreach Plan

**Purpose:** Concrete execution plan for generating the first clients. Turns the distribution and pipeline decisions into weekly actions with specific channels, targets, and messaging.

**Goal:** 2 assessment calls per week within 30 days. That's the bottleneck — everything downstream (proposals, closes) follows from assessment volume.

**Pipeline math:**

| Stage                            | Conversion | Volume needed/year       |
| -------------------------------- | ---------- | ------------------------ |
| Warm touches/week                | --         | 15-20                    |
| Touch → conversation             | 20%        | ~150 conversations       |
| Conversation → assessment booked | 40%        | ~60 assessments          |
| Assessment → proposal sent       | 80%        | ~48 proposals            |
| Proposal → close                 | 25-30%     | ~27 engagements          |
| **Annual target**                |            | **27 engagements @ avg** |

15-20 warm touches per week sustains this. At 25-30% close rate on proposals, 27 engagements per year hits the annual target. The touches are warm: networking, referrals, introductions, Vistage/EO speaking, LinkedIn engagement. Not cold outreach.

## Week 1: Open the Fastest Channels

### Accountant / Bookkeeper Outreach (Decision #22)

This is the fastest path to a warm referral. No event to attend, no membership to buy.

**Action:** Identify 5-10 bookkeepers and small CPA firms who serve growing businesses in the Phoenix metro.

**Where to find them:**

- Google: "bookkeeper Phoenix AZ small business"
- LinkedIn: search by title + location
- ASCPA member directory
- Ask existing professional contacts for names

**The intro message (LinkedIn DM or email, 3-4 sentences max):**

> Hi [Name], I run a solutions consulting team here in Phoenix. We work with growing businesses on the operational stuff that's not financial but makes your clients harder to work with: broken processes, tools that don't talk to each other, the owner stuck in every decision.
>
> When we find a client whose books aren't current, we refer them to a bookkeeper. Figured it might be worth a quick conversation to see if there's a fit the other direction. Would you have a few minutes this week?

**Goal:** Book 3-5 intro calls. These are relationship-building, not a pitch. Explain what you do, ask what their clients struggle with, establish the reciprocal referral.

**The reciprocal:** When we disqualify a prospect for dirty books, we make a warm handoff to a partner bookkeeper. That's a real referral in their direction — not a hypothetical.

## Week 2-4: Build the Recurring Channels

### BNI Chapter

**Action:** Visit one BNI chapter in the Phoenix area. Target the "business consultant" or "operations consultant" seat.

**Why BNI:**

- Structured referral system — once you're in, the chapter actively generates referrals for you
- Takes 60-90 days to build momentum, so start early
- Weekly cadence forces consistent relationship-building

**How to show up:**

- Use the vertical-appropriate one-liner from your intro
- When asked "who's a good referral for you": "A business owner doing somewhere between $750k and $5M who says 'I can't take a day off' or 'our tools don't talk to each other.' Someone who knows the business has outgrown the way things run but hasn't had time to fix it."

## Vistage / EO Channel

### Why Peer Groups Matter

Vistage and EO (Entrepreneurs' Organization) are where business owners in the revenue band talk to each other. A speaking slot at a peer group meeting puts you in front of 12-20 qualified buyers at once, with built-in credibility from the chair's invitation.

### Getting Invited to Speak

The value proposition for the chair: you bring a practical session their members can use immediately. Not a pitch. Not a thought leadership lecture. A working session.

**Outreach to chairs (LinkedIn DM or email):**

> Hi [Name], I run a solutions consulting team here in Phoenix. We work with growing businesses on the operational stuff that breaks when they scale: processes that live in the owner's head, tools that don't connect, no visibility into what's actually happening day to day.
>
> I'd love to bring a working session to your group. The format: we walk through the five things we see most often in businesses at this stage and how to spot them. Members leave with something they can act on that week. Would that be useful for your group?

**Target:** 1-2 Vistage/EO speaking engagements per quarter. Each one is a room full of qualified buyers.

### The Session Format

Keep it practical. No slides if you can help it.

1. Open with a question: "What's the one thing in your business that would break if you took two weeks off?"
2. Walk through 2-3 of the five solution categories with real examples (anonymized client stories when available)
3. Give each person one concrete thing to look at this week
4. Close with an open offer: "If any of this sounds familiar, we start with a conversation. No charge for that."

### Conversion Path

Speaking engagement -> 2-3 members approach after -> assessment call -> proposal. The chair relationship also becomes a referral partner. Nurture chairs the same way you nurture bookkeeper partners.

### Other Peer Groups

- SCORE mentoring events (Phoenix chapter)
- Local CEO roundtables
- Industry-specific mastermind groups

## LinkedIn Thought Leadership

### Cadence

2-3 posts per week. Consistency matters more than volume.

### Content Categories

**Problem recognition (40% of posts):**
What we see in growing businesses. Not sales. Not pitching. Just describing the situation clearly enough that the right person sees themselves in it.

- "If your team texts you every time a decision needs to be made, that's not a people problem."
- "Your CRM isn't broken. You just don't have one."
- "The spreadsheet that runs your business was supposed to be temporary."

**Behind the work (30% of posts):**
What it actually looks like to sort out operations for a growing business. Anonymized, real situations. What we found, what we built, what changed.

**Tools and process takes (20% of posts):**
Practical opinions on tools, integrations, and how to think about technology decisions. Not product reviews. Just honest takes from someone who's configured hundreds of these things.

**Local business engagement (10% of posts):**
Phoenix business community, something a local owner posted that resonated, a pattern you're seeing locally.

### Rules

- No hashtags
- No "Top 5 reasons" lists
- No calls to action in every post. Let the good ones stand on their own.
- Comment on other people's posts more than you write your own
- Share real observations, not manufactured wisdom

## Remote Assessment

### Default: Zoom-First

Assessment calls default to Zoom. The "show me how you do X" part of the call works over screenshare: the owner shares their screen and walks through their actual workflow, their actual tools, their actual spreadsheets. This is where the real signal comes from.

### When In-Person Makes Sense

In-person is optional for Phoenix-area clients, not required. Some situations where it adds value:

- The business has a physical workflow you need to see (warehouse, shop floor, front desk operations)
- The owner is not comfortable with video calls
- The relationship is warm enough that showing up in person builds trust

### How Screenshare Assessment Works

1. Ask the owner to share their screen
2. "Walk me through how you handle [X] right now, start to finish"
3. Watch them click through their actual tools, not what they say they do
4. Take notes on what you see: tab switching, manual data entry, copy-paste between apps, the spreadsheet that runs everything

The screen doesn't lie. You'll learn more watching someone do their job for 15 minutes than from an hour of conversation about their job.

## What "Done" Looks Like

The outreach plan is working when:

1. You have 3-5 referral partner relationships actively referring (bookkeepers, Vistage chairs, fractional CFOs)
2. You're attending 1-2 networking events per week
3. Assessment calls are booking at 2+ per week
4. LinkedIn posts are generating inbound DMs
5. You're not the only source of leads: referrals and inbound are coming in

The constraint is never outreach volume. It's assessment call capacity. If you're getting 2 assessments a week, the outreach machine is working. Focus on converting those into proposals.
