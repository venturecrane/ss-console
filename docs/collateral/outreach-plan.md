# Outreach Plan

**Purpose:** Concrete execution plan for generating the first clients. Turns the distribution and pipeline decisions into weekly actions with specific channels, targets, and messaging.

**Goal:** 2 assessment calls per week within 30 days. That's the bottleneck — everything downstream (proposals, closes) follows from assessment volume.

**Pipeline math (from Decision #25):**

| Stage                            | Conversion | Volume needed/month     |
| -------------------------------- | ---------- | ----------------------- |
| Outreach touch → conversation    | 20%        | ~100 touches            |
| Conversation → assessment booked | 40%        | ~20 conversations       |
| Assessment → proposal sent       | 80%        | 7-8 assessments         |
| Proposal → close                 | 35%        | 6 proposals             |
| **Result**                       |            | **2 engagements/month** |

~25 outreach touches per week sustains this. The touches are warm — networking, referrals, introductions — not cold outreach.

---

## Week 1: Open the Fastest Channels

### Accountant / Bookkeeper Outreach (Decision #22)

This is the fastest path to a warm referral. No event to attend, no membership to buy.

**Action:** Identify 5-10 Phoenix-area bookkeepers and small CPA firms who serve businesses in the 10-25 employee range.

**Where to find them:**

- Google: "bookkeeper Phoenix AZ small business"
- LinkedIn: search by title + location
- ASCPA member directory
- Ask existing professional contacts for names

**The intro message (LinkedIn DM or email, 3-4 sentences max):**

> Hi [Name] — I run a small operations consulting team here in Phoenix. We work with 10-25 person businesses on the stuff that's not financial but makes your clients harder to work with — scheduling chaos, no follow-up process, the owner stuck in every decision.
>
> When we find a client whose books aren't current, we refer them to a bookkeeper. Figured it might be worth a quick conversation to see if there's a fit. Would you have 20 minutes this week?

**Goal:** Book 3-5 intro calls. These are relationship-building, not a pitch. Explain what you do, ask what their clients struggle with, establish the reciprocal referral.

**The reciprocal:** When we disqualify a prospect for dirty books, we make a warm handoff to a partner bookkeeper. That's a real referral in their direction — not a hypothetical.

### Chamber of Commerce

**Action:** Join the Greater Phoenix Chamber + one sub-regional chamber (Scottsdale, Chandler, or Mesa — pick based on where target verticals concentrate).

**What to do:**

- Register for the next mixer or networking event
- Bring the one-pager
- Lead with a problem, not a title: "We work with business owners who've outgrown doing everything themselves but don't have time to fix it."
- The ask is always a conversation, not a sale. Goal: book an assessment call.

## Week 2-4: Build the Recurring Channels

### BNI Chapter

**Action:** Visit one BNI chapter in the Phoenix area. Target the "business consultant" or "operations consultant" seat.

**Why BNI:**

- Structured referral system — once you're in, the chapter actively generates referrals for you
- Takes 60-90 days to build momentum, so start early
- Weekly cadence forces consistent relationship-building

**How to show up:**

- Use the vertical-appropriate one-liner from your intro
- When asked "who's a good referral for you": "A business owner with 10-25 employees who says 'I can't take a day off' or 'leads keep falling through the cracks.' Someone who knows something needs to change but hasn't had time to fix it."

---

### Vertical Association Events

**Action:** Attend one event per vertical in the first 60 days.

| Vertical                           | Association | What to attend                      |
| ---------------------------------- | ----------- | ----------------------------------- |
| Home services (plumbing, HVAC)     | PHCC, ACCA  | Monthly meetings, trade events      |
| Professional services (accounting) | ASCPA       | Networking events, chapter meetings |

These are longer-burn. The goal is visibility and relationship — not immediate leads. Bring the one-pager. Have conversations. Follow up.

## Tracking

Until the client portal pipeline is live, track in a simple spreadsheet or note:

| Field           | Purpose                                                            |
| --------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Name / Business | Who                                                                |
| Source          | Where the lead came from (chamber, BNI, bookkeeper referral, etc.) |
| Last touch      | Date of most recent outreach                                       |
| Status          | Conversation / Assessment booked / Proposal sent / Closed / Dead   |
| Next action     | What happens next and when                                         |

**Weekly health check:**

- **Healthy:** 2+ assessment calls booked or completed, at least 1 proposal out
- **Anemic:** No assessment calls this week, or a proposal sitting 7+ days with no response

---

## What "Done" Looks Like

The outreach plan is working when:

1. You have 3-5 bookkeeper/accountant relationships actively referring
2. You're attending 1-2 networking events per week
3. Assessment calls are booking at 2+ per week
4. You're not the only source of leads — referrals are coming inbound

The constraint is never outreach volume. It's assessment call capacity. If you're getting 2 assessments a week, the outreach machine is working. Focus on converting those into proposals.
