# Client-facing content audit — 2026-04-15

Tracks #377 Audit phase. Follow-on to hotfix #378 (fabricated 3-week schedule
stripped from `src/pages/portal/quotes/[id].astro`). Output is read-only — each
finding names its pattern and a proposed remediation; fixes belong to Track D
(engineer) and Track C (scribe/guardrail).

## Summary

- Total files audited: 18
- Pattern A findings: 33
- Pattern B findings: 9
- AUTHORED strings (verified): 14 classes (line-item description, invoice
  description, engagement.scope*summary, engagement.consultant*_, engagement
  next*touchpoint*_, milestone.name/description, quote/invoice dates, client.name,
  and the surface pill/headline labels driven purely by persisted record state)
- Items requiring Captain decision: 5

**Shape confirmation.** The pre-audit preview (comment
[#4249249466](https://github.com/venturecrane/ss-console/issues/377#issuecomment-4249249466))
estimated ~10× more Pattern A than Pattern B. This audit found 33 A vs. 9 B —
roughly a 3.7× ratio, not 10×. The reason: the audit treated every first-person
sentence about post-signing behavior as a Pattern A finding (e.g., the
portal-welcome email's `"This is where you'll find your project details..."`),
and the SOW PDF template surfaces several Pattern A sentences that the preview
did not walk. The directional claim — Pattern A dominates — holds.

**Scope boundaries.**

- Audit covers `src/pages/portal/**`, `src/components/portal/**`,
  `src/lib/portal/states.ts`, `src/lib/email/templates.ts`,
  `src/lib/email/follow-up-templates.ts`, `src/lib/email/booking-emails.ts`,
  `src/lib/email/resend.ts`, `src/lib/pdf/sow-template.tsx`, and
  `src/lib/pdf/scorecard-template.tsx`.
- Out of scope (not walked in depth): `src/pages/auth/portal-login.astro`,
  `src/pages/auth/verify.astro` (pre-auth, not engagement content);
  `src/lib/pdf/scorecard-template.tsx` below its header copy (data-driven
  dimensions/opportunities are AUTHORED via the scorecard config; it is
  prospect-facing, not client-facing engagement content).
- Pure design/presentation strings with no claim of business behavior ("Sign
  out", "Retry", "Back to proposals", status pills like "Signed" / "Paid") are
  counted as AUTHORED (driven by persisted record state) and not enumerated.

1. **Brand name: "SMD Services".** The venturecrane org transferred ss-console
   on 2026-04-08. "SMD Services" appears as literal source text in at least 18
   locations across portal pages, email templates, and the SOW PDF header /
   signature block. Before any batch remediation, Captain needs to confirm:
   (a) is "SMD Services" still the correct client-facing brand; (b) if yes,
   should it be centralized behind a single `BRAND_NAME` constant so future
   renames are one-line changes; (c) if no, what replaces it. Files touched:
   `src/lib/email/resend.ts:11`, `src/lib/email/templates.ts` (7 occurrences),
   `src/lib/email/follow-up-templates.ts:30`, `src/lib/email/booking-emails.ts:49,77,105`,

   `src/pages/portal/engagement/index.astro:67,76`, `src/pages/portal/documents/index.astro:100`.

2. **Consultant identity: "Scott Durgan" hardcoded as fallback.** Four
   client-facing surfaces default to this exact string when the engagement
   row's `consultant_name` is null: `src/pages/portal/quotes/[id].astro:85`,
   `src/pages/portal/invoices/[id].astro:186`, `src/pages/portal/index.astro:229`,
   and the email signature at `src/lib/email/templates.ts:408-409,555`. This is
   both a Pattern A finding (personal-identity commitment rendered on every
   engagement regardless of actual delivery team) and a Captain-decision:
   should the portal treat `engagements.consultant_name` as required (block
   rendering if null) or render-empty? **Recommended:** require. SMD Services
   CLAUDE.md §6 calls out "we" / "our team" voice; the hardcoded single-name
   fallback runs counter to that positioning.

3. **"Receipt attached" claim in portal UI.** The phrase renders in three
   places (`src/pages/portal/invoices/[id].astro:265,446`,
   `src/lib/portal/states.ts:80`, `src/pages/portal/index.astro:195`). The
   portal does **not** attach a receipt to any rendered surface — only the
   payment-confirmation email does (which is separate: `templates.ts:155`).
   This is a false statement to clients and should be removed from every
   portal surface. Captain confirmation before remediation because the fix is
   simple and removing the phrase changes tone across the payment UX.

4. **SOW PDF fixed-timeframe commitments.** Three places in the SOW template
   hardcode timeframes that may conflict with CLAUDE.md §3 no-fixed-timeframes:
   `sow-template.tsx:517-519` ("within 1 business day of receiving the deposit"),
   `sow-template.tsx:529-532` ("2-week stabilization period"), and
   `sow-template.tsx:570-572` ("within one business day" for kickoff
   confirmation). These are on a signed legal document. CLAUDE.md §3 says
   _external_ content should not publish fixed durations — a signed SOW
   arguably isn't "external content" in the marketing sense; it's a
   contractual commitment where specific timeframes _are_ the point. Captain
   should clarify: does §3 apply to signed SOW PDFs, and if yes, move these
   to per-engagement authored fields; if no, mark them as blessed terms.

5. **"support window" phrase in follow-up email.** `follow-up-templates.ts:211-213`
   uses the phrase `"that's what the support window is for"` in the safety-net
   check-in email. A "support window" is a pricing/scope concept that may or
   may not have been sold in a given engagement. Captain should decide whether
   to (a) remove the phrase; (b) require `engagements.support_window_label`
   authored before the email sends; or (c) treat the email as cadence copy
   that's identical for all post-handoff clients and accept the generalization.

1. Enumerated portal-rendered surfaces:
   - `src/pages/portal/**` — 6 `.astro` pages (`index`, `engagement/index`,
     `documents/index`, `quotes/index`, `quotes/[id]`, `invoices/index`,
     `invoices/[id]`).
   - `src/components/portal/**` — 6 `.astro` components.
   - `src/lib/portal/states.ts` and `src/lib/portal/constants.ts`.
2. Enumerated out-of-portal client-facing surfaces per issue scope:
   - `src/lib/email/templates.ts`, `src/lib/email/follow-up-templates.ts`,
     `src/lib/email/booking-emails.ts`, `src/lib/email/resend.ts`.
   - `src/lib/pdf/sow-template.tsx`, `src/lib/pdf/scorecard-template.tsx`.
   - `src/pages/api/admin/quotes/[id].ts` (templateProps construction — where
     the SOW PDF gets its fabricated strings).
3. For each file, read the full source and enumerated every string visible to
   a client (skipping pure UI chrome — classes, icon names, URL paths, focus
   styles, screen-reader labels).
4. For each enumerated string, traced the interpolation back to its
   originating field. A string was classified AUTHORED only if its source was
   (a) a DB column populated by an admin flow, (b) a config constant
   documented in an ADR / Decision Stack, or (c) strictly-descriptive UI
   chrome that does not make a claim about business behavior. Anything else
   was classified Pattern A (literal sentence implying commitment) or
   Pattern B (literal value rendered from a non-authoritative fallback).
5. Grouped findings by file. For each finding proposed one of REMOVE,
   RENDER-EMPTY, NEEDS-AUTHORED-FIELD (with schema column), or KEEP (with
   rationale). Items that need Captain judgment before remediation were
   surfaced in the "Items requiring Captain decision" section rather than a
   finding-level remediation.
6. Cross-referenced the pre-audit preview (issue comment 4249249466) to
   confirm overlap. The priority-2 item called out by the preview
   (`states.ts:138 We'll reach out to schedule kickoff.`) is captured;
   additional priority items surfaced during this walk (SOW PDF commitments,
   `"Receipt attached"` triplicate, `"Replies within 1 business day"` in the
